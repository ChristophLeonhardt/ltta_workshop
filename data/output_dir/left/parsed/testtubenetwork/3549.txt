Guiyu, China
The city is a dumping site for more than 1
million tons of toxic waste.
Asia creates the largest amount of electronic
waste in the world, with 16 metric tons produced
in 2014.
E-waste is mined for the precious metals found
in electronic products, like gold and copper.
In 2014, recyclable e-waste amounted to $52
billion in resources.
With money comes risk, e-waste is causing
serious environmental and health issues.
The United Nations predicts overall waste
volume will continue to rise with the growth
of the tech industry.
